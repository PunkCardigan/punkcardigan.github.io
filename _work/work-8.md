Professional Development
---
I attended the second day of the Iowa Library Association's Fall Conference in 2024. One session that stood out to me in particular was "Reducing Bias in the Library Catalog: Moving the "Othered" Histories to the 900s" which was presented by Abbi Jensen, a cataloging librarian at the Des Moines Public Library. [link](https://fall2024ila.sched.com/event/1hFY6/reducing-bias-in-the-library-catalog-moving-the-othered-histories-to-the-900s)

**Three things I learned:**
- Many books about people who are part of minority communities find their history being included in the 300s (Dewey's section for works about culture and identity, among other things) instead of the 900s (for works on historical events).
- DMPL worked on a project to move relevant items from the 300s to the 900s, beginning with a digital audit, sorting and otherwise managing the items under consideration, and assigning new call numbers to items that should be moved.
- Dewey is deeply flawed, but there are people (in Iowa!) working on making it a little better.

**Two questions I have:**
- Did the change confuse patrons at all?
- What other sections of nonfiction have bias in their contents?

**One thing I will do differently:**
- When I am cataloging books on historical topics, I will consider their contents and topics carefully to decide whether they should go into the 900s or not.
