This project is a nine minute instructional presentation intended for an academic or public library professional audience. I developed the topic of how to use the library catalog effectively and make searching faster and more efficient, to improve reader's advisory or help patrons use items.

[Presentation on Searching with Biblionix Apollo (PDF)](/files/apollosearchtalk.pdf)
*author's note: This presentation was created in 2022 and Biblionix has since updated the functionality of its software and its documentation. I do not know if any specific criticism is still applicable.*

This was my favorite project in the first class I took after I started considering getting my MLIS but before I was accepted into the program, which is sort of its own example of competence 3, Lifelong Learning and Continuing Education. More specifically for this assignment, subcategories 3A (Participate in and lead on-going professional development to better serve their communities.) and 3C (Employ multiple techniquest to accomodate diverse learning preferences to promote lifelong learning.) stood out to me for this one.

My initial conceptualization of this topic was mainly based around authority records, but the first time through the draft of my presentation, I realized that it was actually 70% about keyword searches and the other 30% was me hedging around a rant about how the authority records in my library’s catalog are a hot mess and even if I had the time to fix them (which I don’t), hoopla uploads a ridiculous amount of garbage authority records along with the new titles to my catalog every few months (and although I have since convinced the director to remove hoopla from the catalog, Bridges/Libby still does the same thing to a slightly lesser extent). I also didn’t think I had enough time to cover the extent of what I had originally planned.

I also used several different databases (including APA PsychInfo, ERIC, Education Source, and Library Lit) and GoogleScholar to find information on making PowerPoint presentations more accessible, especially over Zoom. There isn't a ton of literature out there, but I found that most of it had similar conclusions, although people with different disabilities may have contradictory needs. I incorporated what I could of the suggestions into the final presentation.
