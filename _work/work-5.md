This project involved the creation of an original LibGuide using Springshare's LibGuide contement management system. I chose the topic of Adult ADHD, which I am intimately familiar with.

[View the LibGuide here](https://slis-uiowa.libguides.com/AdultADHD)

[Archive.org link](https://web.archive.org/web/20250418153228/https%3A%2F%2Fslis-uiowa.libguides.com%2FAdultADHD)


From this project, I've gained experience with competence 5, Organization of Recorded Knowledge and Information, specifically 5A (Understand the principles, systems, trends, and goals involved in the organization and representation of recorded knowledge and information.) and 5D (Recognize the ways that cultural biases impact and influence the collection and description of recorded knowledge and information.)

Creating the LibGuide was a fascinating process, because so much of what I know about ADHD is either personal or widely distributed in different locations and mediums. I wasn't always able to find sources for the content I wanted to include, because the metadata wasn't helpful, like with celebrities with ADHD. I also chose to include a lot of videos, because that is often a more accessible format for people with ADHD, even though much of the peer-reviewed information on ADHD isn't available in video or audio format.

A topic that has fascinated me for a while now has been the ways that knowledge organization can affect our perceived reality. We organize information so that it can be easily identified and retrieved when necessary. Unfortunately, the biases in the methods we use can make it difficult or even impossible to retrieve certain information, essentially erasing it from our collective awareness. The organizational choices that we make speak to what information we think is valuable enough to exist and our biases can inadvertently silence groups we weren’t even aware of. As the increase in book bans in the past several years indicates, this can also be done deliberately. Because I am a cataloging librarian and this is the professional area that I am most curious about, I hope that I can take the skills I have learned apply them directly to the decisions I make about catalog records every day.
