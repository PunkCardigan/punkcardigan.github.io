This assignment is a reflection on a group assignment that simulated a Planning Team and the process of proposing an improvement to a library, using several professional frameworks. In this case, we were trying to solve an issue with patrons remaining in a law library after closing.


[Reflection (PDF)](/files/managementreflection.pdf)
---


The subcategories of the Managment and Administration competence that this assignment exemplefies are
- 4B. Apply the principles of effective and just supervisory practices and human resource management, training, and development, and
- 4G. Participate in strategic communication with colleagues throughout the organization and the community.

For 4B, we discussed how library staff wear all sorts of hats, but there are limits to what can be asked and expected of a librarian, so we included other departments (like campus security) into our planning. For 4G, I communicated with a remote group using email, Zoom, and GoogleDocs to sucessfully complete the assignment.

When reflecting on the group assignment, I was able to see that my preference for working in a team is to play a supporting role, rather than a leadership role, since my strengths lie with problem solving and editing. This assignment, and this class in general, also helped me confirm both that I am not particularly interested in an administration job and that management is not as boring as I thought it would be. Even if I don't want a formal position, using leadership and management skills can help with project managment and communication.
